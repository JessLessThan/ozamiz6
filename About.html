About Ozamiz City

Ozamiz City is a first-class city in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines, known for its blend of cultural heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty. Situated at the foot of Mount Malindang and along the shores of Panguil Bay, the city offers picturesque landscapes and a calm environment. The city is historically recognized for Fort Santiago (Cotta Shrine), a Spanish-era fortress and religious site, and is a hub for cultural festivities like the Subayan Keg Subanon Festival, showcasing the rich traditions of the Subanen people. Ozamiz is also a gateway to the Mount Malindang Range Natural Park, a UNESCO-declared ASEAN Heritage Park, teeming with biodiversity. Economically, the city thrives on agriculture, aquaculture, and commerce, serving as a key trading center in Northern Mindanao. With its strategic location, warm community, and commitment to progress, Ozamiz City is a harmonious blend of history, culture, and modern living.

History

Senator Jose Fortich Ozamiz

was renamed Ozamiz City in honor of the late Senator Jose Ozamiz, a man who pioneered underground resistance activities in Misamis Occidental against Japanese colonialists and was imprisoned at Fort Santiago and finally executed at La Loma Cemetery together with other Filipino patriots. Ozamiz has gone a long way from an Old Spanish settlement to its present enviable economic position in the region. It is now emerging as the one of the fastest growing cities in Northwestern Mindanao.

Ozamiz Famous For

In 2024, Ozamiz City remains renowned for its historical, natural, and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore landmarks like the Spanish-era Cotta Fort, the Bamboo Pipe Organ at Immaculate Conception Cathedral, and the historic Bells of Bukagan Hill, all of which reflect the city's rich heritage. Nature enthusiasts are drawn to Mount Malindang Natural Park, offering activities such as hiking, camping, and relaxing in its hot springs and waterfalls, as well as the Misamis Occidental Aquamarine Park, known for its serene environment and marine conservation efforts. Additionally, Ozamiz delights food lovers with unique dishes like *sinuglaw* and local specialties, including *chorizo de Cagayan*, adding to its reputation as a vibrant cultural and culinary destination.
